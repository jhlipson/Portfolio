Feel No Evil – Prototype (December 2025)

Role: Programming Lead & Game Designer

  • Character movement systems
  • Modular weapon architecture
  • Player statistics framework
  • Role-playing mechanics integration
  • Lead programming team on general architecture

Working with a team of 10 individuals, Feel No Evil was the biggest project I have worked on thus far. The following is a rough prototype from two months of consitent development. The project in question is a horror title focused on the player facing down creatures stronger than themselves only to become more powerful the more they face their fears. Use the axe to fight back, with each kill the player's level increases, and their weapon's individual power, independent of the player increases.

My role for the Feel No Evil Prototype expanded over time. I was responsible for all player-character mechanics and for integrating systems developed by other programmers into a cohesive architecture.

I designed a modular weapon system that allowed new weapon types to be added seamlessly as the project evolved. I built the axe combat flow, implemented player statistics, and ensured tight integration between combat systems and the game's role-playing mechanics.

The prototype later transitioned into a more substantial Alpha stage, where these foundational systems supported expanded development.
